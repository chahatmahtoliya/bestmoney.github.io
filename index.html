Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about health insurance

How do I choose the right health insurance plan?

When choosing a health insurance plan, consider your healthcare needs, budget, preferred doctors and hospitals, prescription medications, and coverage for specific services. Compare deductibles, premiums, copays, and out-of-pocket maximums to find the best value for your situation.

What's the difference between HMO, PPO, and EPO plans?

HMO (Health Maintenance Organization) plans typically require you to stay within a network and get referrals from a primary care physician. PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) plans offer more flexibility to see specialists without referrals and some coverage for out-of-network care. EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization) plans combine elements of both, requiring you to stay in-network but not requiring referrals.

Can I change my health insurance plan at any time?

Generally, you can only change health insurance plans during the annual Open Enrollment Period or if you experience a qualifying life event (such as marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage) that triggers a Special Enrollment Period. These periods allow you to enroll in or change your health insurance plan outside the standard enrollment window.

Are preventive services covered at 100%?

Under the Affordable Care Act, most health insurance plans must cover preventive services at 100% with no cost-sharing when provided by an in-network provider. These services include annual check-ups, vaccinations, screenings, and certain counseling services. However, it's always best to verify coverage details with your specific insurance provider.
